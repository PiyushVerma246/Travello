LEGENDARY DESERTS

Explore the vast, shifting wildernesses that have shaped civilizations and inspired explorers for millennia.

The Sahara

Covering 3.6 million square miles, the Sahara is an ocean of sand where dunes can reach 600 feet.

Arabian Desert

The Rub' al Khali is the world's largest contiguous sand desert, a realm of golden peaks and deep silence.

Atacama

A high-altitude Mars on Earth. In some parts of this Chilean desert, rain has never been recorded.

Thar Desert

The "Great Indian Desert" is one of the most densely populated deserts, rich in color and Rajasthani folklore.

Gobi Desert

Stretching across China and Mongolia, the Gobi is a cold desert known for its historic Silk Road fossils.

Mojave

The hottest desert in North America, home to Joshua Trees and the surreal landscapes of Death Valley.

LIFE IN THE ARID

THE DESERT DWELLERS

Far from being barren, the desert is a theater of specialized life that has mastered the art of survival.

The Camel

The "Ship of the Desert" can survive weeks without water, storing fat in its hump for energy.

Fennec Fox

The world's smallest fox species with massive ears used to dissipate heat in the Sahara.

Saguaro Cactus

Standing up to 40 feet tall, these icons can live for 200 years in the Sonoran Desert.

DEEP IN THE DUNES

Experience the ultimate thrill in a customized 4x4. Professional drivers navigate steep ridges and sheer drops on the shifting peaks. It's an adrenaline rush like no other.
